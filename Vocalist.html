The Vocalist

Questions to ask your future vocalist

Below are a list of questions that you should ask your potential vocalist. These questions are designed to get a feel for their experience, dedication, and personality. I cannot tell you what the right answers are or how you should interpret their answers. I can't even tell you that these questions are guaranteed to help. Because I am not a vocalist, some of this information, and probably the most helpful questions, are coming from the subreddit linked on the home page and right before the questions. If you want more detail on why those questions, you can check there. Although, most of the questions are ones that I would ask if I were looking for a vocalist. Also, if I left anything out, please fill out the feedback form linked at the bottom of the page.
Vocalist Reddit

  • How long have you been singing?
  • Do you have any formal vocal training?
  • What are your long-term goals as a vocalist?
  • Do you have your own mic and speaker/pa system?
  • Have you ever performed live? or What is your band history?
  • What is your personal life like? (This is to determine if they have a hectic life that could bleed into the band)
  • What are your favorite bands?
  • What styles of music are you most comfortable singing?
  • How often do you typically practice?
  • What is your usual availability during the week?
  • How well do you handle constructive criticism?
  • Are you open to collaborating on songwriting?
  • What would your ideal band be?
  • If politics could disrupt the band, ask "What kind of benefit shows they feel comfortable playing?" to subtly find out their political views.

Activities

Below are a list of activities to do that could help. However, the only ones that I would consider as required are the ones marked as musical. The musical activities are more thought out, thanks to the reditors on the subreddit linked above. The other activities are fun and good for bonding, but not guaranteed to help. Adjust any of these activities as you see fit because these are just the outlines of what I would do.

Activity Name Description musical or bonding?
Solo Play This activity is the most important and probably the most obvious. Have them play something for you. It could be an entire song, a chorus or breakdown, or just their octave range. Also, let them know to let loose and imagine they are on stage so you can get an idea for their stage presence. musical
Attend a Concert Together This activity is a great way to bond over similar tastes in music. It also gives you a chance to see how they behave in a social setting. Do they seem respectful to others? If you go to a rock or metal concert, do they mosh? If they do, do they follow the mosh etiquette of picking someone up when they fall? This is a good way to tell if they are a good person. bonding
Group Practice This is another crucial activity. You need to play with them to see how well they fit in with the rest of the band. Do they listen to the other musicians, interpret the feel of the song, and adjust their singing accordingly? Do they sing too loud or too soft? Also, run through a typical set twice to see if their voice struggles at any point. Singing is hard work, and if they can't make it through a normal set without sounding tired or losing their voice, then they aren't the right fit. Lastly, record this practice and get an nonmusician's opinion on how they sound. Sometimes it is hard to tell how well they are doing when you are playing with them. musical
Social Hangout (e.g., coffee, dinner, karaoke) This activity is good for getting to know the person outside of a musical setting. It helps to see if you have similar interests and if you can get along on a personal level. Remember, you will be spending a lot of time with this person if they join the band, so it's important to make sure you like them as a person. The activities listed are just suggestions. Pick something that you will all enjoy. Although, karaoke is a great way to find out if they can loosen up, and if they have stage fright that they aren't telling you about. bonding
Songwriting This activity is just for if you write or plan on writing your own songs. Give them a song you have written and ask them to come up with a vocal melody and lyrics for it. This will give you a good feel of what their input into the band will be like. Do they come up with something that fits the feel of the band? musical
Team-building Activities (e.g., escape room, pictionary/charades) These activities are great for bonding and learning how to work together as a team. If they can work with others and if they will work with your bandmates are two different things. Everyone communicates differently, and these activities can help the new person learn how to communicate with the others. Plus, these activities are fun and can help break the ice. bonding
